# Book Fit Assessment Guide

## What Makes a Good Book2Skill Candidate?

**Core requirement:** The book must contain **actionable frameworks, models, or step-by-step methods** that can be extracted into independent, executable skills.

Think of it this way: if the book teaches you *how to do something* (not just *what to think about*), it's a good fit.

---

## High Fit (Recommended)

Books that contain clear, repeatable frameworks with defined inputs and outputs.

| Category | Examples | Why They Work |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| Entrepreneurship & Startups | *The Lean Startup*, *The Minimalist Entrepreneur*, *Zero to One* | Clear methodologies (MVP, build-measure-learn) |
| Strategy & Competition | *Blue Ocean Strategy*, *Good Strategy Bad Strategy*, *Playing to Win* | Structured frameworks (ERRC, strategy canvas) |
| Product & Design | *Hooked*, *Inspired*, *The Design of Everyday Things* | Repeatable processes (Hook Model, jobs-to-be-done) |
| Sales & Negotiation | *Influence*, *Never Split the Difference*, *SPIN Selling* | Tactical frameworks with clear steps |
| Management & Leadership | *The One Thing*, *First Break All the Rules*, *High Output Management* | Actionable management tools |
| Personal Productivity | *Atomic Habits*, *Deep Work*, *Getting Things Done* | Proven systems with implementation steps |
| Decision Making | *Thinking in Bets*, *Decisive*, *Principles* | Decision frameworks with clear processes |
| Growth & Marketing | *Traction*, *Hacking Growth*, *Crossing the Chasm* | Methodical approaches with worksheets |
| Writing & Communication | *On Writing*, *Storyworthy*, *Talk Like TED* | Structured techniques and templates |

**Key traits of high-fit books:**
- Contains 3+ distinct frameworks or tools
- Each framework has clear inputs, steps, and outputs
- Real-world case studies demonstrate application
- Reader can apply the framework without re-reading the book

---

## Medium Fit (Possible with Extra Work)

Books that have valuable content but require more effort to extract actionable skills.

| Category | Examples | The Challenge |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| Business Biography | *Shoe Dog*, *The Everything Store*, *Steve Jobs* | Narrative-heavy, frameworks buried in stories |
| Case-Driven Books | *The Innovator's Dilemma*, *Built to Last* | Theories illustrated through cases, but hard to operationalize |
| Philosophical Business | *Zero to One*, *The Hard Thing About Hard Things* | Strong concepts, fewer step-by-step frameworks |
| Psychology & Behavior | *Thinking Fast and Slow*, *Predictably Irrational* | Descriptive (how people think) vs prescriptive (what to do) |

**What to do:** Extract only the most actionable 2-4 frameworks, skip the narrative parts.

---

## Low Fit (Not Recommended)

Books that cannot be meaningfully converted into executable skills.

| Category | Examples | Why Not |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Fiction & Literature | *1984*, *The Great Gatsby*, any novel | No frameworks to extract |
| Narrative History | *Sapiens*, *Guns, Germs, and Steel* | Storytelling, not methodology |
| Pure Philosophy | *Meditations*, *The Republic* | Abstract thinking, not actionable tools |
| Memoir | *Educated*, *Becoming* | Personal stories without repeatable methods |
| Reference Books | Dictionaries, encyclopedias, cookbooks | Data, not frameworks |

---

## Quick Assessment Checklist

Answer these questions to evaluate any book:

1. **Does it contain 3+ distinct frameworks or methods?** (Not just ideas, but repeatable processes)
2. **Can each framework be used independently?** (Without reading the whole book)
3. **Does it have clear inputs and outputs?** (User provides X, framework produces Y)
4. **Are there real-world examples?** (Case studies that demonstrate application)
5. **Can someone apply it without re-reading?** (The framework is self-contained)

**Scoring:**
- 4-5 Yes = **High Fit** → Proceed with full conversion
- 2-3 Yes = **Medium Fit** → Extract only the best frameworks
- 0-1 Yes = **Low Fit** → Consider a different book

---

## Output Format

After assessment, provide:

```
Book: [Title]
Author: [Author]
Fit Level: High / Medium / Low
Reasoning: [2-3 sentences explaining why]
Recommended Skills: [number] ([list key frameworks])
Suggested Package Name: [book-name]-pro
Should Proceed: Yes / Yes (selective) / No
```

## Input Formats Supported

Book2Skill Factory can work with:
- **PDF** files (text-based, not scanned images)
- **EPUB** files
- **Markdown** or plain text
- **Readable web links** (articles, blog series, online book chapters)
- **Direct text input** (paste book content or detailed notes)

Note: Scanned PDFs without OCR will need to be converted to text first.
